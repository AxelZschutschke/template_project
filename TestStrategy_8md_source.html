17 ## Introduction
18 
19 The test documentation gives an overview of test targets, test scope and
20 defines and explains the internal notations. Also, an overview of the test
21 environment and the test approach is presented.
22 
23 During a release cycle all test related documents are updated according to
24 their natural dependency. The [test strategy](\ref TestStrategy) is the most
25 stable document (time-wise), as it describes general definitions and the long
26 term test approach (strategy) of the organisation. In terms of
27 [ISO 29119-2](\ref TestStrategyRef), it reassembles the OT1 (test specification of the
28 organisation) on project level and may as well be an exact copy of the test strategy of
29 the organisation if applicable.
30 
31 The [test plan](\ref TestPlan) will be adapted to new
32 features and found defects, for the next release while the
33 [test report](\ref TestReport) is virtually subject to change with each new
34 build.

50 ## Scope
51 
52 The test strategy document shall define the goal of all activities related to
53 test and quality. It should align all developers, testers and other relevant
54 stakeholders with regards to a common understanding of quality goals and test
55 related notation. The document shall be reviewed and agreed on by developers,
56 testers and other relevant stakeholders.
57 
58 Testing activities are conducted in a continuous manner, meaning that with
59 every product iteration, the quality gates for releasing the respective
60 increment to customers shall be assessed and be reached prior to shipment. The
61 main goal of the testing activities is to detect errors early, while creating a
62 culture of quality awareness and an open error culture. Error reports are
63 appreciated at any point of the development cycle to enable transparent defect
64 management.

91 Requirements on **technical level** shall be reflected by the review process
92 and the acceptance criteria of development activities. They are created during
93 technical analysis of the problem and software design. There is little to no
94 cutomer gain and feedback for such requirements.
95 
96 Requirements on the **stakeholder level** are based on stakeholder request and
97 describe the features the software product shall include. They are directly
98 evaluated by the stakeholder and should be explicitely tested.
99 
100 **Functional** requirements are usually introduced as feature request,
101 describing the functionality of the software product.
102 
103 **Non-functional** requirements are more delicate, as they are often expected
104 silently by stakeholders. They must be asked explitely from stakeholders and some
105 must be formulated by development.

107 ## Test Approach
108 
109 Facing the requirements of continuous integration, the test process must be
110 adapted. Here, methods of continuous testing will be applied to ensure a high
111 level of quality of the product while keeping the pace of modern development.
112 
113 **Continuos testing** is a pipeline strategy, not bound to dedicated testing
114 phases and with different closed feedback loops. [Zimmerer 2019](\ref TestStrategyRef)

116 ## Legal / Regulations
117 
118 The test procedure also takes legal aspects into considerations, including the
119 (redistributable) license status of all components used, legal regulations for
120 the acceptance and permissions to release the system and the applicance of
121 standard specifications.
122 
123 Special care must be taken for open source software components used in the
124 product, as they have different and potentially very restrictive license types.
125 Also, law is very specific with respect to using these components, as the full
126 test responsibility is remaining by the product using the open source software.

128 ## Test Levels
129 
130 There are four levels of testing considered in this project:
131 * unit test
132 * integration test
133 * sub-system test
134 * system test
135 
136 The amount and intesity of testing together with the level of automation is
137 decreasing from unit to system test level. The higher test levels, e.g.
138 sub-system and system test also include all external components to assure the
139 correct integration and interaction of the product with the environment. The
140 test levels are not bound to software test alone but also include documentation
141 and design as well as all other (testable) artifacts, prepared during product
142 development.
143 
144 ### Unit Test
145 
146 Test of the smallest building blocks of the software. The test focusses on a
147 single method or class, calling a public interface and evaluating its result.
148 Three different types of results can be evaluated (while one test should only
149 evaluate one of the three): the returned value, a measurable change of the
150 state of the system or by the way an other part of the system is called. Side
151 effects and integration effects should be excluded from the unit test, to
152 ensure the consistency and repeadability of the test.
153 
154 The unit test is a developer-oriented test and is usually designed and
155 implemented by the author of the code. An independend review should be carried
156 out to ensure sharpness of the test. Unit tests are completely automated,
157 consistent and fast, which makes them available for regression testing on a
158 daily basis.
159 
160 ### Integration Test
161 
162 The integration test very similar to an unit test. The main difference is that
163 the integration test is not actively avoiding internal dependencies, while
164 still not openly relying on external dependencies. Target is to verify the
165 correct interaction and collaboration of the internal components via their
166 interfaces.
167 
168 ### Sub-System Test
169 
170 The unit and integration test extract parts of the software and create a
171 "virtual" environment for the tests to run in. The sub-system test shall partly
172 overcome this limitation and test the individual packages of the final product
173 as shipped, while still simulating parts of the interaction of other packages
174 and/or hardware.
175 
176 ### System Test
177 
178 The system test shall test the product under real world conditions, with all
179 external and internal components in place and on the target hardware. As this
180 would create an infinite number of test scenarios with different environments
181 and component versions, dedicated test scenarios need to be identified, which
182 shall represent all imaginable combinations.

184 ## Roles and Responsibilities
185 
186 Any team member of the developer team can take on one or more of the following
187 roles for his/her work. Sometimes, it may be beneficial to employ dedicated
188 testers and developers, but often, they may double up on both roles. For high
189 level test, a different person should test the product than the one who implemented
190 it. Also, peer reviews shall protect from soft testing.
191 
192 For details on the role's responsibilities, please refer to the
193 [definition page](\ref Definition).
194 
195 A team consists of one **product owner** and a development team.
196 The development team should staff the following roles:
197 * one **team quality representative**
198 * one **team architect**
199 * several **developers**
200 * several **testers**

208 ## Explorative Testing
209 
210 Next to the thoroughly planned test tasks during sub-system and system test
211 a less restricted approach is explorative testing. This approach is used to
212 find "new defects" by setting the developers, testers or other stakeholders
213 into an unconstrained test scenario. A general topic and state of mind is
214 set to begin with, then a time boxed test session without any rules or concrete
215 test steps is conducted and recorded. This approach allows for a more creative
216 setup and "random" findings, which are triggered by certain chain of events
217 unforseeable or overseen by the test plan. It is beneficial to have a test
218 document with a fixed checklist to query the whole test environment. The list
219 is filled prior to testing, so that in case of an finding, a complete error
220 report can be created with ease and no questions about the actual situation
221 and versions arrise.
222 
223 ## Security Testing
224 
225 Security in terms of data security and ITSec is an crucial part of any modern
226 system. Special care must be taken to ensure the secure and uninterrupted
227 operation of the system, even in case of targeted attacks against the system.
228 Applying a set of secure coding guidelines, which can be enforced by analyis
229 tools and code reviews, can help to reduce attack vectors against the system.
230 The test activities with respect to security shall be conducted by specially
231 trained professionals. The budget and time for this analysis must be planned
232 early in the project.

252 ## Test Tools
253 
254 For different levels of testing, different tools and environments are utilized.
255 For unit- and integration tests, a unit test and isolation framework is used,
256 which provides means for faking parts of the runtime system and creates
257 executable binaries, which internally call the parts of the system under test,
258 which are focussed at by the active test case. They are compiling the test
259 report and execute the test cases in a configurable order (e.g. in random
260 order, to make sure the test cases are independend of each other). Here, the
261 google testing framework is used for these low level tests.
262 
263 For higher test levels, the actual artifacts of the product built are used
264 as test specimens. They are relying on the actual platform they are built for
265 and may lack to "set up" defined test conditions artificially. For this,
266 the artifacts are executed in a virtual (sub-system test) environment or on
267 the actual target (system test), which creates a set of obstacles, especially
268 for test automation.
269 
270 ## Reporting
271 
272 The tools used for testing create a junit-style test result file in xml format.
273 The coverage is presented as raw coverage file, as generated by the coverage
274 tools. The dashboard application and CI chain can read these files to create
275 trends of code coverage, test executed and failed next to the build results.
276 
277 The visualization of test results is done within the documentation of the
278 project. The documentation shall include:
279 * code documentation
280 * architectural documentation (C4 model)
281 * test documentation
282 * active requirements
283 * list of open (known) defects
284 * changelog, incl. closed defects
285 * test report of the current build
286 
287 Using this approach, a complete and archivable artifact is created without
288 external dependencies.

294 # Definition of Done
295 ## Reviews
296 
297 Reviews shall be carried out for all design documentation and all implemented
298 code, be it production or test code. For test code, a walkthrough is sufficient
299 as review, for design documents and production code (inluding the code
300 generated by pair-programming), a dedicated peer review shall be carried out by
301 at least one peer.
302 
303 ## Coverage
304 
305 As the test process has no natural completed critereon, the test coverage is
306 used as end-of test indicator for the low level test types (unit and
307 integration testing). Given reviewed test cases, the coverage becomes a valid
308 measure for the overall status of testing. Both test levels shall cover 100 %
309 of statements and at least 100 % of branches. Modified condition/decision
310 coverage is used as measure for code of highest integrety requirements, which
311 is not the case here, thus basic branch coverage is a sufficient measure.
312 
313 The higher test levels are measured based on the requirements. Here, a
314 requirement coverage is defined, and set to 100 % as target coverage criterion.
315 That means that all requirements applicable for a given software release shall
316 be tested by at least one test scenario, specifically designed for this
317 requirement. Requirements may be tested on lower test levels, which would make
318 a re-test on system level optional.
319 
320 ## Automation
321 
322 The project aims for a high degree of test automation. For unit and low-level
323 integration testing, full automation shall be reached (100% of tests). For
324 higher test levels, individual test cases might be hard or impossible to
325 automate. For these, a detailed instruction and questionaire shall be created
326 to ensure repeatability and expressiveness of the test result. The test then
327 may be carried out manually, following the questionaire. Overall, 80 % of test
328 cases shall be automated.

341 ## Defect management
342 
343 The defect management includes all steps from adding newly found defects to the
344 defect tracking tool, the defect triage, fixing the source location, retesting
345 and accepting the fix. Defects are prioritized in the defect triage. Here, also
346 the scope of the fix (or the termination of the defect) are decided as well as
347 a time schedule. The defect will be rated regarding its severity. For defects
348 of medium and high severity, the release documentations need to be adapted, for
349 announcing the issue to users.
